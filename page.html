Worst Bracket Wins

Everyone chases perfection. We’re rewarding chaos. Submit your public ESPN Tournament Challenge bracket link — and the entrant with the lowest total score at the official conclusion of the Tournament wins $3,000.

No entry fee. No wagering. One bracket. One winner.

Submit Your Bracket How It Works

Quick summary: Create a bracket on ESPN Tournament Challenge, make it public, and paste your link into the entry form. The winner is determined objectively by the final score shown on ESPN after the Tournament ends.

This is a promotional contest. There is no purchase necessary, no entry fee, and the contest does not involve betting or wagering.

Prize

  • One (1) Winner
  • $3,000 USD cash prize
  • Winner = lowest total bracket score at the official conclusion of the Tournament (as reflected on ESPN Tournament Challenge)
  • Tie-breaker: earliest eligible submission timestamp
  • Winner may be required to complete standard payment and tax verification steps

🥴 What counts as “worst”

The bracket with the lowest total score when the Tournament officially ends.

✅ Objective scoring

Scores come directly from ESPN Tournament Challenge. No public voting. No subjective judging.

🔒 Bracket lock

Once the first Tournament game begins, brackets are locked on ESPN and can’t be changed.

🧾 One entry

One (1) submission per person via the official entry form.

How to Enter

  1. Create your bracket on ESPN Tournament Challenge and make it public.
  2. Submit one (1) entry via the official form and include your public ESPN bracket link.
  3. Keep your bracket public and viewable through April 6, 2026.

Eligibility

  • Open to legal residents of the 50 U.S. states and Washington, D.C.
  • Must be 18+ at the time of entry
  • No purchase necessary
  • One (1) bracket submission per person
  • Employees of Action Network / Better Collective and affiliated companies are not eligible

Scoring & Winner Selection

  • Bracket scoring follows the official scoring system displayed by ESPN Tournament Challenge.
  • Final scores are taken directly from each entrant’s public ESPN bracket link at the official conclusion of the Tournament.
  • The Winner is the entrant with the lowest total score.
  • In the event of a tie for the lowest score, the earliest eligible submission timestamp wins.
  • Action Network’s decisions regarding eligibility, scoring, and winner determination are final and binding.
